Introduction

This portfolio explores the 74-year history of Formula 1, analyzing how the sport has evolved from a niche European championship into a global spectacle. Through five interactive visualizations, we examine the geographical expansion of the circuits and the dynastic battles between the sport's greatest constructors and drivers. The project demonstrates the "Eras of Dominance" in F1, showing how teams like Ferrari, McLaren, and Mercedes have risen and fallen over decades.

1.1 Formula one Circuits

Context & Question

This visualization shows all Formula 1 tracks that have ever existed throughout the history of the championship (1950-2024). Most importantly, it shows a historical shift from a Euro-centric series into a true global spectacle. It is important because this expansion reflects the commercial and political strategy of the sport, moving into new markets in the Middle East, Asia, and the Americas over the last 74 years.

Design Decisions

Mercanter projection was chosen because it is the easiest to interpret for a global map, since the main goal is to show world distribution of the tracks.

Key Insights

The map reveals a dense cluster of large, dark-colored bubbles in Europe (e.g., Monza, Silverstone, Spa). This confirms that despite recent global expansion, the historical core and the majority of races hosted remain deeply rooted in Europe

Data Source: Formula 1 World Championship 1950-2023 (Kaggle)

1.2 Formula one European Circuits

Context & Question

While the previous map shows global distribution, this one fails to capture the density of the sport's heartland, Europe ‚Äî the "spiritual home" of Formula 1. This visualization zooms in to resolve the "overplotting" issue of the global view and examine the specific geography of the sport's heartland.

Design Decisions

Focused Geospatial Map centered specifically on coordinates [15, 53] (Central Europe) with a high zoom scale of 600. This allows us to clearly see the dense cluster of circuits

Key Insights

The visualization confirms that F1‚Äôs historical center of gravity lies firmly in Western Europe. We observe a strong correlation between geography and longevity: UK, Germany, Italy, Belgium, Netherlands and Monaco hosts the largest bubbles, representing circuits with 50+ races

Data Source: Formula 1 World Championship 1950-2023 (Kaggle)

3 The Eras of Dominance

Context & Question

Formula 1 is defined by dynasties. How has the balance of power shifted between manufacturers over the decades? This visualization moves beyond total counts to show the "market share" of victories in every single season.

Design Decisions

I used a Normalized Stacked Area Chart (Streamgraph). By normalizing the Y-axis to 100%. This allows for a fair comparison of dominance. I applied the authentic team livery colors to make the eras instantly recognizable to fans (e.g., the "Red Era" of Schumacher, the "Silver/Teal Era" of Hamilton).
